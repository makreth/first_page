Flavors of Singapore

Hainanese Chicken

Flavorful and fluffy Hainanese Chicken Rice, cooked to perfection with an authentic recipe simmering with Singaporian tradition. A classic comfort food, our restaurant chefs work hard to translate all of the unique flavors exactly as you'd find them back home. Comes with braised, dark soy hard boiled eggs, chicken liver, chicken soup, a bright, spicy dipping sauce and a ginger-garlic dipping sauce.

Satay Bee Hoon

The Singaporian take on rice vermicelli, Satay bee hoon is a longstanding fusion of the vibrant cultures found in hawker centers, first invented by the Teochew immigrats of Singapore. With Singapore's distinct cultures living close together, many new delicacies emerged. This dish is served with spicy peanut satay sauce, chicken skewers, pork slices, and bean sprouts.

Black Pepper Crab

Invented by the famous Long Beach Seafood Restaurant in Singapore back in 1965, the black pepper crab is the quintessential Singaporian seafood delicacy. Messy, indulgent, and filled with spectacular flavors, this dish involves a hard-shell crab fried to perfection with a generous flavoring of black pepper and fresh jackfruit sauce. Comes with rice, serves four.
